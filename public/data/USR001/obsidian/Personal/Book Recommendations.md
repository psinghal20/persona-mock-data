# Book Recommendations

Curated book recommendations organized by genre and purpose. See also [[Personal/Reading List]] for tracking progress.

## Must-Read Classics

### Fiction Essentials
Timeless works everyone should experience.

- **To Kill a Mockingbird** by Harper Lee - A gripping tale of racial injustice and childhood innocence in the American South
- **The Great Gatsby** by F. Scott Fitzgerald - A tragic story of wealth and obsession during the Jazz Age
- **Pride and Prejudice** by Jane Austen - A witty comedy of manners about love and social standing in Regency England
- **Crime and Punishment** by Fyodor Dostoevsky - A psychological exploration of guilt and redemption in 19th century Russia
- **One Hundred Years of Solitude** by Gabriel Garcia Marquez - Multi-generational magical realism at its finest

**My Pick**: *One Hundred Years of Solitude* - The multi-generational story of the Buendia family is unlike anything else.

### Russian Literature
For those ready to dive deep.

- **Crime and Punishment** by Fyodor Dostoevsky - More accessible entry point
- **The Brothers Karamazov** by Fyodor Dostoevsky - Dense philosophical fiction exploring faith and morality
- **War and Peace** by Leo Tolstoy - An epic chronicle of Russian society during the Napoleonic Wars
- **Anna Karenina** by Leo Tolstoy - A tragic love story set against Russian high society

**Start with**: *Crime and Punishment* - More accessible before tackling the longer works.

## Science Fiction & Fantasy

### Epic Fantasy
For when you want to lose yourself in another world.

- **The Lord of the Rings** by J.R.R. Tolkien - The definitive epic fantasy (1,216 pages)
- **The Hobbit** by J.R.R. Tolkien - Perfect entry point (300 pages)
- **A Game of Thrones** by George R.R. Martin - Noble families vie for the Iron Throne
- **The Name of the Wind** by Patrick Rothfuss - A legendary hero tells his story
- **The Way of Kings** by Brandon Sanderson - Epic fantasy of war and heroism (1,007 pages)

**Reading Order for Tolkien**: Start with *The Hobbit*, then *The Lord of the Rings*.

### Science Fiction Classics
Mind-bending explorations of technology and society.

- **1984** by George Orwell - A chilling prophecy about totalitarianism
- **Brave New World** by Aldous Huxley - A darkly satiric vision of utopia gone wrong
- **Dune** by Frank Herbert - Epic saga of politics, religion, and ecology on a desert planet
- **Foundation** by Isaac Asimov - The story of preserving knowledge during galactic collapse
- **Neuromancer** by William Gibson - The groundbreaking cyberpunk novel

**Must Read**: *Dune* - Essential before watching the films.

### Cyberpunk
The genre that predicted our digital future.

- **Neuromancer** by William Gibson - Defined the cyberpunk genre
- **Snow Crash** by Neal Stephenson - Predicted the metaverse decades before Meta

### Fun Sci-Fi
When you want something lighter.

- **The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy** by Douglas Adams - Comedic sci-fi adventure
- **Ender's Game** by Orson Scott Card - A child trained to command humanity's fleet

## Dystopian Fiction
Cautionary tales for our times.

- **1984** by George Orwell - Feared what we hate would destroy us
- **Brave New World** by Aldous Huxley - Feared what we love would destroy us
- **The Handmaid's Tale** by Margaret Atwood - A totalitarian theocracy built on subjugation
- **Never Let Me Go** by Kazuo Ishiguro - A haunting story of friendship at a boarding school
- **The Road** by Cormac McCarthy - Father and son journey through devastated America

**Contrast Reading**: Read *1984* and *Brave New World* back-to-back to compare visions.

## Thrillers & Mystery

### Page Turners
Perfect for travel or weekend binging.

- **Gone Girl** by Gillian Flynn - A twisting thriller about disappearance and guilt
- **The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo** by Stieg Larsson - Journalist and hacker investigate a disappearance
- **The Da Vinci Code** by Dan Brown - Symbologist unravels a conspiracy in Da Vinci's artwork
- **And Then There Were None** by Agatha Christie - The original locked-room mystery

**Classic Mystery**: *And Then There Were None* - Christie at her best.

### Horror Masters

- **The Shining** by Stephen King - Psychological horror at its finest
- **It** by Stephen King - Friends confront ancient evil (1,168 pages)
- **Frankenstein** by Mary Shelley - The original science fiction novel
- **Dracula** by Bram Stoker - The classic vampire tale that defined the genre

**King Starter**: *The Shining* - Perfect entry to Stephen King.

## Non-Fiction

### Self-Improvement
Books that can change your life.

- **Atomic Habits** by James Clear - Practical framework for tiny changes with remarkable results
- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** by Daniel Kahneman - Understanding the two systems of thought
- **The Alchemist** by Paulo Coelho - A shepherd boy's journey to find his personal legend

**Start Here**: *Atomic Habits* - Practical, actionable, immediately useful. See also [[Personal/Health Goals]].

### Business & Startups
Referenced in [[Projects/MCP Atlas Project]].

- **The Lean Startup** by Eric Ries - Continuous innovation for entrepreneurs

### History & Big Ideas

- **Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind** by Yuval Noah Harari - Humanity's creation and evolution

## Adventure & Classics

- **The Count of Monte Cristo** by Alexandre Dumas - The ultimate revenge story (1,276 pages)
- **Les Miserables** by Victor Hugo - Redemption and revolution in 19th century France
- **Moby-Dick** by Herman Melville - Captain Ahab's obsessive quest
- **Don Quixote** by Miguel de Cervantes - Adventures of an idealistic knight
- **The Odyssey** by Homer - Odysseus returning home after the Trojan War

**Epic Adventure**: *The Count of Monte Cristo* - Betrayal, imprisonment, and elaborate payback.
