# Book Notes

## DDIA
*Designing Data-Intensive Applications* by Martin Kleppmann

### Chapter 1: Reliable, Scalable, and Maintainable Applications
Key concepts:
- **Reliability**: System works correctly even when faults occur
- **Scalability**: System can handle growth (data, traffic, complexity)
- **Maintainability**: System can be worked on productively over time

### Chapter 2: Data Models and Query Languages
- Relational vs Document models
- Graph-like data models
- Query languages: SQL, MapReduce, Cypher

### Chapter 3: Storage and Retrieval
- Log-structured storage (LSM trees)
- B-trees
- Column-oriented storage

### Chapter 4: Encoding and Evolution
- JSON, XML, Binary formats
- Thrift, Protocol Buffers, Avro
- Schema evolution

### Chapter 5: Replication
- Leaders and followers
- Replication lag
- Multi-leader and leaderless replication

## Staff Engineer
*by Will Larson*

### Key Takeaways
1. Staff engineers work on the most complex problems
2. Technical leadership != management
3. Sponsor and mentor others
4. Write strategy documents
5. Stay technical but expand scope

### Archetypes
- Tech Lead
- Architect
- Solver
- Right Hand

### Quotes
> "Staff engineers are the folks who push forward the most complex technical problems while simultaneously keeping the organization functioning."

## The Phoenix Project
*by Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford*

### The Three Ways
1. **Flow**: Optimize for speed of delivery
2. **Feedback**: Shorten feedback loops
3. **Continuous Learning**: Culture of experimentation

### Four Types of Work
1. Business projects
2. Internal projects
3. Operational changes
4. Unplanned work

### Key Lesson
Technical debt compounds. Address it systematically.
